Turbo Jet Engine

 Project Description:

 This project was inspired by famous youtuber Colin Furze and his various turbo jet projects. The turbo jet in this project consists of a turbocharger, combustion chamber, and oil system. The turbocharger was found at a local junk yard, the oil system was constructed using common plumbing materials and the combustion chamber was constructed with 1/16 inch thick 304 stainless steel. Propane was used as an atomized propellant. The high-level design and function of each of these components is detailed below:

 TurboCharger
 When starting this project, the first item that should be acquired is the turbocharger itself. This is because the geometry of the combustion chamber depends on the inlet diameter of the turbojet (this is described in the video linked in the combustion chamber section).
 The turbocharger's function is to intake air through the intake turbine, send it through the commbustion chamber and expel the exhaust gases out the exhaust turbine. Both the intake and exhaust turbines are directly connected via a turboshaft. Ideally, a turbo jet of this construction should be able to self sustain the rotation of both turbines after being primed with a leaf blower. Once primed, the exhaust gases generated from the continuous combustion process should be enough to perpetuate the rotation of the exit turbine.  Combustion Chamber
 This video acted as a guide through most of the design and manufacturing decisions of the combustion chamber. The function of the combustion chamber is to direct intake air and fuel into its chamber where they mix in a vortex fashion. Once mixed, a sparkplug or match ignites the mixture and continuous combustion takes place. The exhaust gases are then directed out the exit turbine and thrust is generated.  Oil System

 Future Work:

  As shown in the video below, the turbo jet functioned properly when provided . There are several possible reasons for why the pulse jet did not work exactly as intended. The geometry of the pulse jet is not perfect. This is likely because Dan Brogan does not own a sheet metal bender capable of bending thick stainless steel sheet and had to bend all parts by hand. This led to imperfect geometry. This project is also Dan's first attempt at TIG welding, and there are likely imperfect welds that affect the performance of the pulse jet. Dan is currently working on forming a new combustion chamber that will hopefully improve the performance of the pulse jet.
